VISION:

A learner-centered, inclusive, and empowering education system that continuously improves to protect and promote the right to quality, accessible, relevant, and liberating basic education for all.

MISSION:

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where:

  • Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment.
  • Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner.
  • Administrators and staff, as stewards of the institution, ensure an enabling and supportive environment for effective learning to happen.
  • Family, community, and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share responsibility for developing life-long learners.

FOREWORD

Welcome to Tilambo Elementary School! This handbook is designed to serve as a comprehensive guide for all our students, parents, and guardians regarding the policies, rules, and regulations that govern our school community. It outlines the standards of conduct expected from our students and the support systems in place to ensure a safe, nurturing, and conducive learning environment.

This handbook is developed in strict alignment with the principles and provisions of the Department of Education (DepEd) Orders and Policies, particularly DepEd Order No. 40, s. 2012 (Child Protection Policy) and Republic Act No. 10627, also known as the Anti-Bullying Act of 2013, including its Implementing Rules and Regulations. We believe that by working together, we can foster a culture of respect, discipline, and academic excellence, ensuring that every child feels safe, valued, and empowered to learn.

The safety and well-being of our students are paramount. The following policies are implemented to ensure a secure and orderly environment for everyone:

‚ñ∂

‚ñ∂

Restricted Entry:

‚ñ∂

Entry into the school premises during non-class days (Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays) is strictly prohibited unless expressly authorized by the school administration for official purposes.

Appropriate Attire for Visitors:

‚ñ∂

C. CULTURE OF SERVICE EXCELLENCE AND FINANCIAL POLICIES

Tilambo Elementary School adheres to the highest standards of service excellence and transparent financial management:

Free Basic Education:

‚ñ∂

In accordance with the Philippine Constitution, it is hereby declared to all school stakeholders that basic education at Tilambo Elementary School is provided free of charge.

Prohibition of Fee Collection:

‚ñ∂

Teachers are strictly prohibited from collecting any dues, fees, or contributions from pupils for any purpose whatsoever, in compliance with relevant DepEd Orders on No Collection Policy (e.g., DepEd Order No. 41, s. 2012, and subsequent issuances).

PTA Collections:

‚ñ∂

Only duly recognized PTA Officials shall have the authority to collect any voluntary dues from parents, provided that:

  • The proposed amount and purpose of the dues have been approved by the majority of parents in a general assembly.
  • Contributions are strictly voluntary in nature, and no student shall be deprived of any school service or privilege due to non-payment of PTA dues, in adherence to DepEd guidelines (e.g., DepEd Order No. 54, s. 2009).

Donation Policy:

‚ñ∂

Teachers, PTA, and other stakeholders are not allowed to accept donations for the school without the prior knowledge and explicit approval of the Principal. A designated committee, officially formed and approved by the Principal, shall be responsible for transacting and accepting donations on behalf of the school.

Documentation of Donations:

‚ñ∂

All donations received shall be properly documented. A Certificate of Donation will be issued by the School Property Custodian/Brigada Coordinator, with the endorsing signature of the Principal.

Adherence to Rules:

‚ñ∂

All other school rules stated or posted within the school vicinity shall be carried out honestly, politely, and with integrity.

Feedback and Dialogue:

‚ñ∂

Suggestion box will be available on a designated area to accommodate suggestions and concerns from the school community. Discussion session between teachers and concerned PTA officials will be held monthly/quarterly to address feedback and foster collaboration.

Child-Friendly Environment:

‚ñ∂

All teachers are expected to be gender-sensitive, ardent child protection policy advocates, and to consistently promote a child-friendly learning environment.

A. CLASSIFICATION OF VIOLATIONS AND SANCTIONS

1. LIGHT OFFENSES

‚ñ∂

A Light Offense is defined as an act that is (1) contrary to the general rules and regulations of the school as stated in this Student Handbook or (2) disruptive in nature, but does not result in serious physical injury or significant damage to property.

Examples of Light Offenses:

  • Using vulgar language, insulting remarks, or name-calling not falling under the definition of bullying.
  • Simple Disobedience to directives issued by teachers and other school authorities.
  • Laughing boisterously or shouting excessively inside school premises.
  • Loitering, running in corridors, path walks, and unauthorized areas within the campus.
  • Improper use of the School Uniform or non-compliance with the prescribed dress code (e.g., unkempt uniform).
  • Improper Grooming (e.g., disobeying the prescribed haircut, wearing excessive accessories not aligned with school policy).
  • Littering or disobeying school waste segregation rules.
  • Throwing light objects that could potentially affect or cause minor annoyance to others.
  • Giving inappropriate aliases.
  • Eating others' food without their knowledge or permission.
  • Laughing at others' mistakes.
  • Hiding someone's belongings (e.g., slippers, books, bags).
  • Obstructing or disturbing others during class or school activities.
  • Bringing toys or objects that, while not inherently dangerous, may cause distraction or minor disruption.

Sanctions for Light Offenses:

Sanctions for Light Offenses are designed to be corrective and rehabilitative, in line with DepEd's positive discipline approach.

  • FIRST OFFENSE: Verbal Reprimand and Positive Discipline Session by the class adviser with the student. This session will focus on explaining the impact of the student's actions and guiding them towards appropriate behavior. Parental notification will be made.
  • SECOND OFFENSE: Behavioral Contract. An agreement will be formalized between the student, parents/guardian, and a school representative (e.g., guidance counselor, school head). This contract will clearly state the expected behavior, specific goals, and the consequences if the same offense is violated again. A guarantor (e.g., barangay official, trusted community leader) may be involved.
  • THIRD OFFENSE: Community Service within the school premises or barangay. An agreement will be signed between the student, parents/guardian, and a guarantor (e.g., barangay official) outlining the nature and duration of the community service. The service will be supervised and rehabilitative in nature.
  • FOURTH OFFENSE: Supervised Community Service, with increased duration or intensity compared to the third offense. The specific terms will be determined by the school administration based on the student's progress and commitment.
  • FIFTH OFFENSE: Referral back to parent/guardian for three (3) school days with developmental learning intervention. The student will undertake assigned learning activities at home, supervised by parents, with guidance from the school. This aims to provide a focused period for reflection and behavioral modification.

2. LESS GRAVE OFFENSES

‚ñ∂

A Less Grave Offense is defined as a willful disregard of school rules and regulations that results in less serious physical or emotional harm, or minor material damage.

Examples of Less Grave Offenses:

  • Using profanity or vulgar, racist, or sexually inappropriate language to verbally abuse another pupil, which may escalate towards bullying behavior.
  • Engaging in fights or inflicting physical harm against another student, resulting in minor injuries (e.g., bruises, scratches), not rising to the level of grave physical assault or severe bullying.
  • Threats to inflict a wrong upon the person or property of another person or on his or her family, causing fear or apprehension.
  • Deliberate destruction or minor damage to school property (e.g., school furniture, equipment, fixtures) or personal belongings of other students.
  • Unauthorized alteration or defacing of school documents or records (e.g., report cards, forms).
  • Cutting Classes without valid permission.
  • Extortion, which involves extracting money or property from a pupil or student through non-violent intimidation or subtle pressure.
  • Academic Dishonesty (e.g., cheating during quizzes or minor examinations, copying assignments, minor plagiarism).

Sanctions for Less Grave Offenses:

  • FIRST OFFENSE: Behavioral Contract. An agreement will be executed between the student, parents/guardian, and a broader support network which may include a barangay official, a school counselor, a priest, or any trusted community member who can provide support. This contract will include a clear plan for behavioral improvement.
  • SECOND OFFENSE: Supervised Community Service. The nature and duration of the community service will be proportionate to the offense and designed to foster a sense of responsibility.
  • THIRD OFFENSE: Referral back to parents/guardians for five (5) school days with intervention and referral. The student will be referred to the school counselor or other appropriate professionals (e.g., social worker, psychologist) for targeted support and intervention during this period.

3. GRAVE OFFENSES

‚ñ∂

A Grave Offense is defined as any act that is a gross and willful disregard of school rules and regulations that may result in serious physical injury, significant material damage, emotional trauma, or violations of law. These offenses warrant a formal investigation by the Child Protection Committee or appropriate school authorities, ensuring due process.

Examples of Grave Offenses:

  • Committing theft or assisting in the preparation thereof, involving items of significant value, inside or outside school premises.
  • Possessing or consuming any alcoholic drinks or beverages while in school premises or during official school activities.
  • Membership in any organization or association that espouses violence, illegal activities (e.g., fraternity, sorority, gang), or recruitment of members for such organizations.
  • Gambling in any form inside the school premises leading to significant financial loss or disruption.
  • Selling, possessing, or being under the influence of any illegal or controlled drugs or substances, or possessing drug paraphernalia.
  • Engaging in any form of voyeurism (e.g., secretly recording or viewing others in private situations), posting indecent or lewd photos/videos on the internet, or possessing pornographic materials.
  • Assaulting or physically harming teachers, school staff, or other persons of authority within the school community.
  • Possessing or bringing deadly or prohibited weapons (e.g., knives, firearms, prohibited sharp objects) inside the school premises.
  • Engaging in lewd or inappropriate conduct towards other students, teachers, or other authorities (e.g., public display of affection that is sexually suggestive, sexual harassment).
  • Engaging in fights or inflicting severe physical harm against another student, requiring medical attention or causing significant injury.
  • Extortion, involving extracting money or property from pupils or students through force, severe threat, or intimidation.
  • Academic Dishonesty on major assessments (e.g., cheating on summative examinations, stealing examination papers, malicious alteration of official school records).

Sanctions for Grave Offenses:

For Grave Offenses, the school will conduct a thorough investigation, ensuring due process, and implement disciplinary actions in strict accordance with DepEd Order No. 40, s. 2012 (Child Protection Policy), Republic Act No. 10627 (Anti-Bullying Act), and other relevant DepEd policies on student discipline. Sanctions may include:

  • FIRST OFFENSE: Rendering supervised community service for five (5) days to the school or barangay, coupled with intensive intervention and referral to the school counselor, psychologist, or relevant external agencies if applicable. A behavioral contract will be formalized.
  • SECOND OFFENSE: Referral back to parents/guardians for three (3) school days. During this period, the student will engage in structured developmental learning interventions facilitated by school guidance personnel and parents.
  • THIRD OFFENSE: Referral back to parents/guarda
  • FOURTH OFFENSE: Referral back to parents/guardians for an extended, specified number of days, which will be determined in consultation with parents/guardians, school officials, and relevant professionals. The duration will depend on the severity of the offense, the outcome of interventions, and the need for further support or referral. This may involve longer periods of absence from school, with a clear reintegration plan. In extreme cases, and after exhausting all possible interventions and following due process, severe disciplinary actions, including exclusion or expulsion, may be considered in accordance with DepEd regulations.
